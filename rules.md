# WALL WARS — Game Rules

## Goal

Be the first player to get **10 points**. You score points by closing rectangles with your walls.

---

## The Board

- The board is a **6×6 grid** with **7×7 dots** (intersections)
- The **edges of the board** work like natural walls — both players can use them to close rectangles
- You place walls on the **lines between dots**, just like in the game Dots and Boxes

---

## Players

There are **2 players**, each with a different colour.

---

## How to Start (Setup)

The game begins with these steps, in this exact order:

1. **Random pick:** the game decides randomly who is Player A and who is Player B
2. **Player A** picks their starting position — they click on a dot on the edge of the board (you **cannot** pick a corner)
3. **Player B** picks their starting position — they can see where Player A is, so they can choose to stay close or far away (corners are **not allowed** here either)
4. Now the game begins! **Player A** takes the first normal turn

> **Why this order?** Player A gets to move first, but Player B can choose their position after seeing where A is. This makes the game more fair.

---

## How a Turn Works

On your turn, you **must choose one** of these two options:

### Option 1 — Roll the Dice and Place Walls

1. You roll a **3-sided dice** (the result is 1, 2, or 3)
2. The number you roll tells you **how many walls** you must place

**Rules for placing walls:**

- Your very first wall must start from your **starting dot**
- After that, every new wall must **touch one of your existing walls** (they must share a dot)
- The walls you place in one turn **do not** need to be next to each other — they just need to be connected to your wall network
- You **cannot** place a wall where there is already a wall (yours, your opponent's, or a block)
- You **must** place exactly the number of walls the dice shows — not more, not less

### Option 2 — Place a Block

1. You place **one block** on any free line on the board
2. The block **does not** need to be connected to your walls — you can put it anywhere

**What does a block do?**

- It **cannot** be used by anyone to close rectangles or score points
- It **fills** that space, so nobody can put a wall there
- It is a **defensive tool** to stop your opponent

**How to use blocks:**

- Put it on the line your opponent needs to finish their rectangle
- Put it **inside** a rectangle your opponent is building — this ruins the whole rectangle, even if the border is complete

---

## How Rectangles Work

A rectangle is **complete** when every line on its border is one of these:

- **Your own wall**, or
- **A board edge** (the natural wall around the grid)

**Important rules:**

- If a **block** is on any line of the rectangle's border, the rectangle is **not** complete
- Your **opponent's walls** do not help you — only your own walls count
- If there is a **block inside** the rectangle (on any line inside the area), you **cannot** score it — even if the border is fully closed

**Example:** If you build along the top edge of the board, you only need **3 walls** to close a 1×1 square, because the board edge is the fourth side.

---

## Scoring

When you close a rectangle, you get **points equal to its area** (the number of small squares inside).

| Rectangle | Points |
| --- | --- |
| 1×1 | 1 |
| 2×1 | 2 |
| 2×2 | 4 |
| 3×2 | 6 |

- Each rectangle only gives you points **once**
- If you close a big rectangle that has smaller rectangles inside (that you already scored), you only get points for the **new big rectangle** — you do not count the small ones again
- You get the points **right away** when you close the rectangle

---

## How the Game Ends

The game ends when a player reaches **10 points**. That player **wins immediately**.

---

## Discussion Notes

1. **Who goes first?** — Right now the game picks randomly, but another idea is to have both players roll a dice and the higher number goes first.
2. **Do walls block the opponent?** — Yes! Your opponent cannot use your walls to close their rectangles. So every wall you place also works as a natural obstacle for them.
3. **Other powers** — In the future, we could add special abilities or power-ups to make the game more interesting (for example, different types of blocks, or the ability to remove a wall).
